## Referensi

Daftar pustaka berikut memuat literatur ilmiah berupa buku dan artikel jurnal akademik terkemuka yang menjadi rujukan dalam menganalisis hubungan antara sepak bola, geopolitik, kekuatan lunak (*soft power*), dan *sportswashing* sepanjang sejarah penyelenggaraan Piala Dunia FIFA.

1. **Boykoff, J. (2022).** Toward a theory of sportswashing: Mega-events, soft power, and political conflict. *Sociology of Sport Journal*, *39*(4), 342-351. [https://doi.org/10.1123/ssj.2022-0095](https://doi.org/10.1123/ssj.2022-0095)

2. **Brannagan, P. M., & Giulianotti, R. (2015).** Soft power and soft disempowerment: Qatar, global sport and football's 2022 World Cup finals. *Leisure Studies*, *34*(6), 703-719. [https://doi.org/10.1080/02614367.2014.964291](https://doi.org/10.1080/02614367.2014.964291)

3. **Brannagan, P. M., & Giulianotti, R. (2018).** The soft power-soft disempowerment nexus: The case of Qatar. *International Affairs*, *94*(5), 1139-1157. [https://doi.org/10.1093/ia/iiy163](https://doi.org/10.1093/ia/iiy163)

4. **Chadwick, S., Widdop, P., & Goldman, M. M. (2024).** *The geopolitical economy of football: Where power meets politics and business*. Routledge. [https://doi.org/10.4324/9781003473671](https://doi.org/10.4324/9781003473671)

5. **Grix, J., & Brannagan, P. M. (2016).** Of mechanisms and myths: Conceptualising states' "soft power" strategies through sports mega-events. *Diplomacy & Statecraft*, *27*(2), 251-272. [https://doi.org/10.1080/09592296.2016.1169739](https://doi.org/10.1080/09592296.2016.1169739)

6. **Sbetti, N. (2020).** Was football fascist? The 1934 World Cup in the postwar memory. *Soccer & Society*, *21*(7), 819-833. [https://doi.org/10.1080/14660970.2020.1793624](https://doi.org/10.1080/14660970.2020.1793624)

7. **Scharpf, A., Gläßel, C., & Edwards, P. (2023).** International sports events and repression in autocracies: Evidence from the 1978 FIFA World Cup. *American Political Science Review*, *117*(3), 909-926. [https://doi.org/10.1017/S0003055422000958](https://doi.org/10.1017/S0003055422000958)
